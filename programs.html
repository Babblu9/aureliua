Program Overview

The SAT tests mathematical reasoning, evidence-based reading, and writing at a level that reflects college-readiness. Our preparation goes beyond drilling practice questions. We build the conceptual foundations that allow students to tackle unfamiliar problems with confidence.

Evidence-Based Reading and Writing

Students develop close reading skills for complex literary, historical, and scientific texts. Writing modules cover grammar, punctuation, style, and rhetorical analysis as tested on the SAT.

  • Passage analysis across fiction, social science, natural science, and history
  • Vocabulary in context and inference skills
  • Grammar and usage: commas, modifiers, parallel structure, subject-verb agreement
  • Rhetorical analysis: purpose, tone, and evidence evaluation

Advanced Mathematics

The SAT Math section covers algebra, problem-solving, data analysis, and advanced topics including trigonometry and complex numbers. Our faculty teach each concept from first principles before applying it to test conditions.

  • Heart of Algebra: linear equations, inequalities, systems
  • Problem Solving and Data Analysis: ratios, percentages, statistics
  • Passport to Advanced Math: quadratics, polynomials, exponential functions
  • Additional Topics: geometry, trigonometry, complex numbers

Test Strategy and Mock Practice

Students sit full-length timed mocks under exam conditions from the third week of the program. Each mock is followed by a detailed review session identifying error patterns and pacing issues.

  • Minimum six full-length practice tests
  • Error log methodology for targeted review
  • Time management and pacing strategies
  • Official College Board materials and adaptive practice

Why IELTS Matters

IELTS is the most widely accepted English proficiency test for university admissions in the UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand, and is increasingly accepted by US institutions. A strong IELTS score is both a prerequisite and a signal of academic readiness.

Listening and Reading

Our listening modules train students to extract information accurately from lectures, conversations, and monologues. Reading modules develop skimming, scanning, and inference skills across a range of text types.

  • Note completion, multiple choice, and diagram labelling practice
  • Passage comprehension: True/False/Not Given and matching headings
  • Academic vocabulary building across disciplines
  • Timed reading with speed and accuracy tracking

Writing: Task 1 and Task 2

Writing is where most students lose significant marks. Our writing curriculum is structured around band descriptors, giving students a clear framework for what examiners reward.

  • Task 1: Describing graphs, charts, diagrams, and processes
  • Task 2: Argumentative, discussion, and problem-solution essays
  • Cohesion, coherence, lexical resource, and grammatical range
  • Weekly written submissions with detailed faculty feedback

Speaking: Fluency and Confidence

Speaking lab sessions are conducted in small groups with structured feedback after each attempt. Students are recorded and reviewed to track improvement across the three-part speaking test format.

  • Part 1 fluency practice: personal and everyday topics
  • Part 2 cue card methodology and idea generation
  • Part 3 discussion: abstract and analytical responses
  • Pronunciation, coherence, and vocabulary range coaching

Why AP Courses Strengthen Applications

AP scores signal college readiness before a student sets foot on campus. A score of 4 or 5 in a relevant subject demonstrates mastery that admissions offices at competitive universities actively look for. Many universities grant course credits for strong AP scores, allowing students to accelerate their degree.

Integrated Management Pathways

The Integrated Programme in Management Aptitude Test (IPMAT) grants admission to five-year integrated management programmes at IIM Indore, IIM Rohtak, IIM Ranchi, and several other premier institutions. Succeeding requires strong quantitative ability, verbal reasoning, and a compelling interview performance.

Quantitative Ability

  • Arithmetic: percentages, ratios, profit and loss, time and work
  • Algebra: equations, inequalities, functions
  • Number theory: factors, HCF, LCM, remainders
  • Geometry: circles, triangles, coordinate geometry
  • Data Interpretation: graphs, tables, caselets

Verbal Ability and Logical Reasoning

  • Reading comprehension at speed with accuracy
  • Vocabulary, fill-in-the-blanks, and sentence correction
  • Logical reasoning: sequences, syllogisms, puzzles
  • Verbal reasoning and para-jumbles

Personal Interview Preparation

  • Interview technique, body language, and structure
  • Current affairs and general awareness preparation
  • Why management, why this institution — response coaching
  • Mock interviews with detailed faculty feedback
